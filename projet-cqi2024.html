About The Competition

Description

The CQI Senior is an annual competition of robotic where 12 universities each send 1 team of 4 members try to make the best robot. This challenge involves knowledge of electronics, mechanics and programming. The competition started at 20h a thursday and finished 12h later. Over this time, each team have to make the best robot with the material given, but also a presentation explaining their methodology.

Description of the Challenge

This year, the Senior challenge took place on a spaceship that had suffered an electrical failure. 5 challenges could be completed by the teams to earn points: Climb a slope, Simon's says, turn a knob, flip a switch and move batteries.


My role

As the Director Senior, my role was to create the challenge. So I wrote the rulebook of the competition and I also created the terrain. The construction of the feild has required several hours of wiring, but also of programming in order to operate each of the modules. Fortunately, some friends helped me make all the 3D models present on the challenge while another helped me debug my modules.
